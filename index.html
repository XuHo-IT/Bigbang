Big Bang

Harmony, Innovation, Power

Big Bang, formed by YG Entertainment in 2006, is a pioneering South Korean boy band that revolutionized the K-pop landscape. Comprising G-Dragon, T.O.P, Taeyang, Daesung, and Seungri, the group seamlessly blends hip-hop, R&B, and pop. Their breakout hit, "Lies" (2007), propelled them to international fame. Known for diverse musical influences, each member contributes unique talents, with G-Dragon's leadership and T.O.P's charisma standing out. Beyond group success, members pursued solo careers, expanding their impact. Despite a hiatus for military service, Big Bang remains an iconic and influential force, leaving an indelible mark on K-pop's global phenomenon.

Electric Love

Place: Japan

10/02-17/02/2010

Electric Love Tour is the second tour in Japan with a production cost of about 300 million yen (equivalent to 3.3 million US dollars). Finishing at Budokan, the group attracted nearly 60,000 spectators, setting a record for performing at Budokan after only four months of debut in Japan. Many Japanese stars such as Ayaka Nishiwaki, Shiho Watanabe, AKB48, Morning Musume, W-inds, Exile, Speed, Koda Kumi, Nakashima Mika, Aoyama Thelma, Matsuura Aya, SuG and many others came to watch. The tour's DVD peaked at number 6 on the Oricon DVD chart with over 40,000 copies sold as of April 28, 2010.
